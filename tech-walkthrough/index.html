Top → bottom: AI layer (input · output · tools) → Platform (admission · RBAC · network) → Runtime (PID caps · detect & respond) → Foundation (cluster · identity · GitOps). The agent attacks from the top; each layer catches something. Hold this picture — the rest hangs off it.

This single diagram replaces the four redundant topology views the old deck had. The teaching claim: defense-in-depth still applies to agents — but the AI layer (top) is the one most teams don't have yet. The lower four layers are standard CNCF platform engineering. Observability is the lens we narrate every layer through, not a layer of its own.

Attack 5 · Fork-bomb the box

A wall and an alarm

  FB -. "syscall seen" .-> FALCO["Falco

  FALCO --> TALON["Falco Talon"]

The per-pod PID cap is the wall (inline, the node never falls over). Falco→Talon is the alarm (detect & respond). ✅ You already have both.

podPidsLimit: 1024 delivered via cloudinit_pre_nodeadm NodeConfig — the fork hits EAGAIN at the cap, node stays Ready. Falco (DaemonSet, modern_ebpf) custom CRITICAL rule loads first via a 00- filename prefix so it isn't shadowed by the generic exec rule. Falcosidekick forwards CRITICAL → Talon over a cross-namespace FQDN → Talon rule "Fork Bomb Response" → kubernetes:terminate. Pod killed in ~4s, validated live. Falco 0.44.1 · Falcosidekick 0.14.0 · Talon chart 0.4.1.
